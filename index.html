What services do MSPs manage or provide?

MSPs are a one-stop shop for tech. It's a common business model to encounter when seeking IT services. Services are provided to varying degrees across three levels:

Management: technology consulting and infrastructure integration

Operations: dedicated handling of core services

Helpdesk: end-user desktop support

Because they are so broad and in-depth, business relationships with MSPs are long-term and tightly intertwined. Contracts are on a monthly, quarterly or annual basis with projects and incidentals billed in addition.

MSPs also package value-add services (VAS), which are resold 3rd party products (often branded with the MSP's logo). Web hosting, cybersecurity validation, remote desktop software and VoIP phone services are common value-adds. The MSP benefits from the resale profit and the ability to present itself as a larger, more diversified organization. You benefit by having these services managed on your behalf by a single party.

What is break/fix? What is a proactive or preventative model?

Break/fix is a reactive model that responds to problems on a case-by-case basis. Like an emergency plumber or electrician, break/fix technicians make repairs quickly and bill you for time and materials. Because the success of a pure break/fix model relies on frequent breakages, their technicians are usually on the road all day. High volume is the name of the game. And because all work is incidental, you only pay as-needed.

This contrasts with a proactive maintenance model, where problems are viewed as symptoms of a root cause and regular work is undertaken to strengthen entire systems. Proactive maintenance is often performed in scheduled downtime windows or after-hours as part of an ongoing service contract. Occasional breakages are unavoidable, so most MSPs perform a mixture of break/fix and proactive work.
