What services do MSPs manage or provide?

A managed services provider is a one-stop shop for all things tech and is a common business model to encounter when seeking IT services. MSPs typically operate on a monthly recurring revenue (MRR) basis, although many operate on a pure "break/fix" model. Because they generally manage everything, business relationships with MSPs are long-term and tightly intertwined.

MSPs usually offer value-add services (VAS). A value-add service is a service that the MSP does not directly provide, but resells from a 3rd party. Web hosting, cybersecurity compliance, remote desktop connections and VoIP phone services are all common VAS packages. The MSP derives additional profit from the resale, but they manage the service on your behalf. As a single point of contact, an MSP can save you a lot of time and energy if you don't have an internal IT structure.

What factors should inform my choice between these different options?

That question is exactly why we're here. Your business journey has probably required wearing a lot of hats and trusting your instincts. Nobody expects to be conversational (let alone competent) in every specialized field. We want you to focus on your profession.

If you're aware of inadequacies in your technology stack, but you don't know what "good" looks like, you've come to the right place.

Maybe you aren't ready to establish a service contract with an MSP, or maybe you plan to keep your IT in-house by hiring a systems administrator. Perhaps you're comfortable managing some of your own services in-house and only need occasional help with a project, like an office buildout.

You might have had a negative experience with a break/fix company, or found that you didn't use your MSP's services enough to balance the cost. Companies which only provide break/fix support rely on your systems failing to make money. It isn't in their business interest to teach you how to fix it yourself. MSPs typically build their own internal documentation on your systems for their techs to use. This documentation isn't immediately accessible to you, or in a format you would find helpful. Some MSPs are much better than others, and handing off control from one MSP to another can be painful. Onboarding and offboarding processes tend to be on the scale of months. Playing roulette is no fun.

With an in-house IT team, you hold the keys.
